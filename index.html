The Concept

When you hear the brand “skinnymamas” what comes to mind?

This image?

Exactly. That’s why we avoid instant recalls. It’s easy to draw something like this and pass it off as the mascot for the brand, but the project brief said that the brand evokes witty.

The identity should be smart and witty. It doesn’t need to be too loud like these:

We opt for a more subtle and unassuming approach to our identity.

The idea of the identity is a play on I being fit and I am thin,

That’s why the identity doesn’t have a mascot or a representative. Instead, we focus on “i”.

The I is skinny
The I is thin
The I is slim
The I is fit.

We want the customer to realize that the I is them, without being to blatant about it.

Instead of going for a logomark, we opt to go with a logotype, it’s an efficient way to communicate your message without letting the audience think. This is a form of branding that most fashion agencies employ.

A parent's love is eternal and stays with the child forever.

Typography

Our chosen typeface is superfamily, Rockeby made by MyCreativeLand. It’s tall and slender structure lends itself to be compressed without sacrificing legibility.

For our body type, we employ Whitney Narrow, a subset of Whitney family by Hoefler & Co.

Energetic, Lively and active. Were the main themes that we use to come up with the color scheme. Lime green, coupled with the leafy green give the the brand a natural image.

Bright orange and lemon yellow evoke freshness and hype.

The salmon pink to connote femininity, the brand after all is for moms aged 30 to 50.
